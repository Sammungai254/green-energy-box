Features

Green Energy Box

The Green Energy Box has been developed as a concept to contribute to a low-carbon future by decarbonizing industrial and manufacturing processes. It is designed as a co-generation Combined Heat and Power (CHP) plant solution for the simultaneous production of electricity and process heat using renewable energy sources. Specially designed to serve agro-processing and other industrial enterprises, the Green Energy Box delivers baseload power, 24/7, 365 days a year.

Two Configurations

Available as Biomass-fired or Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) versions to fit diverse industrial needs.

Three Energy Products

  • Electricity
  • Heat for thermal processes
  • Cooling solutions

Innovative Service Models

Offered as Energy as a Service (EAAS) and Cooling as a Service (CAAS) to reduce upfront costs and complexity for clients.

Modular and Scalable

Small Scale: 3–25MW / 0.5–5MWe
Large Scale: 25–100MW / 1–20MWe

No Upfront Capital

Delivered on an EPC, Build, Own & Operate basis, the Green Energy Box remains the property of Green Planet International. Clients only pay for the metered energy consumed, minimizing risk and complexity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about the Green Energy Box, its technology, business model, and applications.

1. What is the Green Energy Box?

The Green Energy Box is a co-generation Combined Heat and Power (CHP) plant solution that simultaneously produces electricity, heat, and cooling. It comes in two versions: biomass/biomass wastes-fired and concentrated solar power (CSP).

2. What types of energy products does it provide?

The Green Energy Box delivers three outputs:
• Electricity
• Heat for industrial and thermal processes
• Cooling solutions for commercial and industrial applications.

3. How does the business model work?

It is offered on an Engineering, Procurement, Construction (EPC), Build, Own, Operate basis. Clients do not make upfront capital investments; instead, they only pay for the metered energy consumed, reducing financial risk and complexity.

4. What are EAAS and CAAS in this context?

EAAS (Energy as a Service) and CAAS (Cooling as a Service) are flexible economic models where clients subscribe to energy or cooling capacity rather than investing in their own infrastructure.

5. Who are the potential clients?

Potential clients include:
• Firms with simultaneous electricity and heat demand
• County Aggregation Industrial Parks (CAIP)
• Export Processing Zones (EPZ)
• Special Economic Zones (SEZ)
• Agriculture, forestry, and livestock processing industries
• Commercial and Industrial Parks.

6. What capacity ranges are available?

The Green Energy Box comes in two scalable modules:
• 3MW–25MW/0.5MW–5MWe (Small Scale)
• 10MW–100MW/1-20MWe (Medium Scale)
